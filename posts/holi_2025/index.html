Holi celebration in Bellevue
March 15, 2025 · 2 min · 254 words · Khushi | Suggest Changes

Holi, the festival of colors, is one of the most vibrant and joyous festivals in India, celebrated to mark the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil.

This is festival has roots from the Hiranya Kashyapu legend. King Hiranyakashipu, a demon king, was granted a boon that made him nearly invincible and wanted everyone to worship him. His son Prahlad was a devotee of Lord Vishnu which angered him to no end. Enraged, the king plotted to kill Prahlad and sought the help of his sister Holika, who had a magical cloak that made her immune to fire. Holika tricked Prahlad into sitting on a pyre with her, but by divine intervention, the cloak flew off her and covered Prahlad instead. Holika burned to ashes, while Prahlad was saved. This event symbolizes the victory of righteousness (dharma) over evil, and the eve of Holi is marked by Holika Dahan (a bonfire) to commemorate this.

“Bura na mano, Holi hai!” - a popular saying as people smear colors over their friends and family! The Bellevue event was held at Crossroads park and had quite a turnout despite the cloudy cold weather. There was a DJ playing popular songs. Everyone were dancing and it was a happy colorful vibe!

The throwing of colored powder symbolizes unity and the shedding of inhibitions, creating a mesmerizing spectacle of hues. Lots of kids running around and trying to out color their peers haha! We had fun throwing different shades of gulal and dancing our hearts out.
