Bellevue

My city
February 15, 2025 · 2 min · 358 words · Khushi | Suggest Changes

This Valentine’s day, I want to talk about my city - Bellevue!

Nestled in the heart of the Pacific Northwest, Bellevue, Washington, is a city that perfectly blends urban sophistication with natural beauty. Located just across Lake Washington from Seattle, Bellevue has rapidly grown into a thriving hub for technology, business, and outdoor enthusiasts.

Downtown Bellevue is amazing especially during the nights. Lots of happening places and just beautiful lighting with shopping. Crossroads for all things Asian and Indian. I went to Rasoi and Dosa House for Indian cuisines and both were good for North Indian and South Indian focused food respectively. There are a lot of parks around and the public transport is good enough to get by to most places.

One of the most stunning aspects of Bellevue’s landscape is the breathtaking mountain views visible from downtown. On clear days, I can see:

The Cascade Mountains – Stretching across Washington, the Cascades provide a stunning backdrop, especially when the peaks are covered in snow.

Mount Rainier – Towering at 14,411 feet, this iconic stratovolcano is one of the most majestic sights from Bellevue, dominating the southern skyline.

The Olympic Mountains – To the west, across Puget Sound, the Olympic Mountains add to Bellevue’s picturesque views, creating stunning sunset silhouettes.

I also visited Everett which is north of Bellevue. It’s a quiet residential area and mostly a suburb with lot of Boeing employees as its residents. It has its own rustic charm!

Snoqualmie – Nestled in the foothills of the Cascade Mountains, Snoqualmie is best known for Snoqualmie Falls, a breathtaking 268-foot waterfall that attracts visitors year-round. The town also offers outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and skiing at Snoqualmie Pass, making it a perfect getaway for nature lovers.

Redmond – Known as the “Bicycle Capital of the Northwest” and home to Microsoft’s headquarters, Redmond is a thriving tech hub with a strong cycling culture, beautiful parks, and a charming downtown. The city is also famous for lot of Indian restaurants and Costco :P

Next on my list to explore is Bridle trails once the weather gets better and the National Parks in and around here!
